Perspective
Things I Believe
Inspired by Lee Robinson. These are mine — formed from shipping in production, learning on the bus, and still figuring it out.
On shipping
Shipping fast beats the best strategy
A working prototype beats a perfect pitch deck
I built their website before the interview. That's the only answer.
Speed is a superpower — especially at 24 hrs/week
On AI
AI is a goldmine in a gold rush — imagination and execution are the only constraints
Talk to your family about AI. Especially your mum. If she gets it, you're building the right thing.
AI didn't replace my thinking — it upgraded it
AI-native engineers will move 10x faster than those unwilling to change
On learning
You'll learn more talking to engineers on the bus than from most courses
Rejection is just a dataset — iterate anyway
Get 1% better every day
Grit > talent. Every time.
There's no substitute for putting in the hours
The best engineers say "I don't know yet" more than anyone
On business & engineering
"This website doesn't matter if you have no revenue from it."
Understand the problem before you touch the keyboard
The business doesn't care how elegant the code is — it cares if it works
Landings > launches — adoption > shipping code
Listen, build, ship, tell the customer, then repeat forever
On communication
Clear writing is clear thinking
Communication is the job — the code is just the output
Mismatched expectations lead to sadness
Say the right thing, to the right people, at the right time
On being a forward deployed engineer
Your constraints are your advantage
Being the outsider gives you the clearest view
You can just change your mind when you're wrong
Have strong opinions, loosely held
Leadership means owning outcomes beyond the org chart
Influence > titles
On the long game
You have no career ceiling
Work can be your hobby — this doesn't mean you can't have other hobbies
Be authentic and own your failures publicly
Being helpful compounds
Still learning. Still growing. Still making mistakes worth documenting.
