The Problem

A QLD seafood leader with a fishing fleet, 2 retail stores, wholesale ops and transport — running on an old Shopify store with no unified tech infrastructure.

The Hustle

Built their website before the interview. Walked in and said: "This website doesn't matter if you have no revenue from it." Got the job. First production role — international student, 24 hrs/week.

What I Built

Eliminated Shopify (saving $20k+/yr). Custom full-stack e-commerce: dynamic pricing, RBAC (super admin / admin / staff / wholesale), inventory system, iOS delivery app, transport booking app, AI stock pipeline.

The Hard Parts

No production experience. Learned by talking to IBM, Amazon & CrowdStrike engineers. Navigated Australian privacy law, PCI DSS, human resistance to change, and my own tendency to ship fast without documenting.

Perspective
Things I Believe
Some learned the hard way. Some still being tested. All of them are mine.
On AI
For people like me, AI is a goldmine in a gold rush. Imagination and execution are the only limits.
The best way to understand AI isn't a course. It's a dinner table conversation with your family.
If you can explain what you're building to your mum and she gets excited — you're building something real.
AI didn't make me a developer. It made me a faster thinker.
On shipping
Shipping fast beats the best strategy. The market tells you more than any planning session.
I built their website before the interview. That's still the best decision I've ever made.
A working demo says more than a fifty-slide deck.
Landings > launches. Adoption matters more than shipping.
On learning
You learn more from one real conversation than from ten tutorials.
Talk to your family about what you're building. You'll understand it better yourself.
Rejection is just a dataset. Iterate anyway.
Grit > talent. Every time.
On business & engineering
"This website doesn't matter if you have no revenue from it."
Understand the workflow before you touch the code.
The business doesn't care how elegant the code is. It cares if it works.
Revenue validates everything. Features validate nothing on their own.
On being you
Your constraints are your advantage. Pressure creates shape.
Own your failures publicly. It builds more trust than any portfolio.
Have strong opinions, loosely held.
Still learning. Still growing. Still making mistakes worth documenting.
